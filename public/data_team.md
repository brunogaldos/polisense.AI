polisense:
What do energy planners in emerging economies like Jordan, Moldova, and Peru have in common? They're all trying to build clean energy infrastructure — BUT ARE stuck in the same bottleneck.
Here is the problem, choosing where to build THAT infrastructure, means manually pulling satellite imagery, census and grid data across fragmented systems, then writing a 50-page report just to unlock execution. This takes months and expensive consultants. Enterprise tools cost millions and free tools require experts.
That’s why we built Polisense — an AI platform where planners ask questions in plain language, get geospatial insights on an interactive map, and generate regulatory-reports aligned with ministry templates — in minutes, not months
Our MVP is live and showing already results. In Peru’s Ministry of Energy, we cut planning time by 80%. We have more than 20 active users, and LOIs from Jordan, Mozambique and Moldova. Winning the AI Energy competition in New York organized by the Global Covenant of Mayors sparked this idea and gave us access to more than 13,000 cities — a $2.4 billion market.
